Abstract
Steganography is an important covert communication technique. Existing steganography schemes are static, relying on fixed embedding logic and exhibiting predictable steganographic behavior, which makes them vulnerable to unknown attacks. To enhance the resistance of steganography against unknown steganalysis, a Steganographic Moving Target Defense Framework with Dynamic Behavior Concealing (StegMTD-DBC) is proposed for the first time. Dynamism is provided to break the constraints of the fixed scheme and significantly increases the diversity of samples that attackers must gather, making it challenging to construct a stable feature space, thereby continuously raising the attack cost. We decouple steganography into two processing links: message embedding and behavioral concealing, where behavioral concealing uses variable neural networks to keep concealment and dynamics simultaneously, enabling continuous and dynamic update or switch of the behavioral concealing network, introducing unpredictability into the system. The decoupling strategy provides full backward compatibility, accommodating both traditional and neural network-based static approaches. We theoretically demonstrate that our framework can achieve a one-time pad-like communication scheme, where a different behavior concealing neural network is employed for each session, without any synchronization with the receiver. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed framework significantly raises the challenges and costs for potential attackers, and effectively enhances the security of the steganographic system while maintaining high-quality reconstruction of the carrier.
